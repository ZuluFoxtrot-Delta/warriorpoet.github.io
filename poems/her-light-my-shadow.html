Her Light, My Shadow

← Table of Contents
Some nights, I tuck her in and wonder how much of my quiet she already understands. She laughs loud, loves big, and doesn’t flinch at small things. And I pray she never learns why that matters. I walk through this life with ghosts at my six and promises in my chest, but she only sees my hands— steady, gentle, made for building, not breaking. She asks if I’m okay. I say yes. And most days, it's close to true. Because I choose to feel it all, to break the cycle, to let the fire stop with me. I wear the scars, so she doesn’t have to.
