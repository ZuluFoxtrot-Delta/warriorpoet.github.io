PTSD

playing with my head. My mind. My heart. My soul. Like it’s bored. Like it’s got time to kill and I’m the only target left. So I put on normal. I wear it like a uniform. Smile when it’s required. Nod at the right moments. Laugh on cue. Show up. Produce. Function. Pretending to be normal. Pretending not to care. Pretending I don’t feel my body tighten up at nothing. Pretending the silence doesn’t sound like a warning. Lying to myself “It’s just a dream.” “It’s just a memory.” “Just a life.” Like I can label it small and it will shrink. Like I can call it “past” and my nervous system will believe me. But it doesn’t. It toys with me. It drags the old footage out of storage and plays it on the inside of my skull like a punishment. Making me see things that aren’t there but they feel real. Making me hear sounds that don’t exist anymore but my body reacts like they do. Nothing false. Nothing real. Just a blurred line between what happened and what’s happening now. Because PTSD doesn’t show you the past like a story. It drops you back into it like a trapdoor. Everything I’ve already seen. Already heard. Already lived. A replay in my head. Never ending. Never ceasing. No mercy. No credits rolling. No off switch. And the worst part? I can be standing in my kitchen, in my own home, in my own country, and still feel like I’m not safe. I can be surrounded by peace and my body will still search for war. Still scan corners. Still listen for impact. Still brace for a hit that isn’t coming. My chest tightens like something is about to happen. My jaw locks like I’m getting ready to absorb it. My hands remember violence even when I’m only holding a coffee cup. I tell myself I’m fine. But fine is just what I say so nobody asks questions I don’t know how to answer. Fine is the lie I learned to keep the world comfortable while I’m drowning in my own bloodless battles. PTSD doesn’t just haunt me it recruits me. It makes me relive. Rehearse. Recalculate. Re-run missions that will never be completed. And sleep… Sleep used to be rest. Now it’s an ambush. Sleep is a tormentor now a doorway I hesitate to walk through because I know what’s waiting on the other side. The nightmares don’t come gently. They kick in the door. They grab me by the throat. They don’t ask permission. They don’t explain themselves. They just drag me under. Trapping me in my nightmares where time doesn’t obey rules and danger is always close enough to taste. I wake up exhausted from fighting wars that don’t leave bruises but still leave damage. I wake up with my heart sprinting like it ran from something my eyes never saw. I wake up angry at a body that won’t stand down. At a mind that won’t stop scanning. At a soul that can’t find quiet even in a room full of safety. And then I do it again. I get up. I shower. I put on the face. I go to work. I answer questions. I make decisions. And nobody sees the part of me that spent the entire night trying not to die inside my own head. That’s what PTSD is. Not weakness. Not drama. Not a story. It’s a war that followed me home and learned my name. It’s living in a body that refuses to believe the danger is over. It’s surviving something once and then surviving it again every night in pieces. And still somehow I keep going. Not because it’s easy. Not because I’m okay. Because I’m still here. And being here is the hardest mission I’ve ever had to complete.
