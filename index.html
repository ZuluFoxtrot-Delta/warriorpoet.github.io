War

0 poems

Combat remembered as residue, what follows you home, settles into the nervous system, and rewrites sleep, silence, and vigilance long after the fighting ends.

  • The Breach
    Sleep becomes a threshold you don’t cross without contact.
  • The Forever War
    The battlefield moves inside and refuses to redeploy.
  • The Clock Doesn’t Tick
    Time turns to drip when your body won’t stand down.
  • Flashbang
    Pain hits like an ambush, instant, total, and loud in the spine.
  • Darkness
    The night isn’t empty, it’s active terrain.
  • From the Bottom of the Well
    The descent strips the lies and leaves only truth.
  • Down the Rabbit Hole (V2)
    Memory becomes gravity, and you fight to come back up intact.
  • In the Silence of the Night
    Quiet turns into a negotiation with what still follows you.
  • PTSD
    A war that followed you home and learned your name.

Fatherhood

0 poems

Not sentimentality, responsibility. A mission measured in presence, patience, and the quiet work of making a child feel safe inside the world.

  • The Good Stuff
    Fatherhood doesn’t fix you, it makes you honest.
  • If I Were to Die Tomorrow
    A legacy message written with no room for denial.
  • Her Light, My Shadow
    A child’s brightness beside the weight you don’t show her.
  • Her Backpack
    What she carries, and what you refuse to add to it.
  • The Mission Rewritten
    Success redefined: trust, gentleness, and staying close.

Leadership

0 poems

Leadership stripped of rank and noise, discipline turned inward, accountability without an audience, and standards held when nobody’s watching.

  • Tactical Leadership Writings
    A collection of inward command: calm, clarity, and consequence.
  • Command and Control
    Own everything. Scan the field. Adjust fire before impact.
  • Orders from Within
    Discipline as scaffolding, the mission stays live.
  • The Quiet Orders
    Routines speak louder than volume when storms hit.
  • Doctrine in the Dust
    The rules you built after the manuals stopped working.
  • Brothers in Arms (for Parker)
    Brotherhood as the one bond the war couldn’t break.
  • The Paradox of Peace and Violence
    Peace isn’t harmlessness, it’s controlled power.

Interior Work

0 poems

The unseen labor: identity, vigilance, memory, and healing, not erasing the past, but putting it in order so it doesn’t drive.

  • Self
    Not conquest — command.
  • Message of Impermanence
    Nothing stays, including what you think never will.
  • A Word to Light
    One true word becomes an anchor in the dark.
  • Against the Tide
    Staying upright when the current wants you back.
  • Clearer Things
    Clarity earned after the fog lifts.
  • The Breath That Remembers
    The body keeps score, and still keeps you alive.
  • The Screams
    When silence sounds like danger, you learn a new rhythm.
  • The Real Standard
    Strength without hardness, stay reachable.
  • Watchtower
    Operational isn’t healed, it’s present and standing watch.
